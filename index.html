Academic Tool Overview

Research Purpose: This tool is designed for educational and academic research in health informatics and data analysis.

Methodology: Body Mass Index (BMI) is calculated using the standard formula: weight (kg) / [height (m)]²

Analysis Process:

  1. Statistical Analysis: BMI calculation with WHO classification
  2. Data Interpretation: Detailed health assessment with academic context
  3. Personalized Guidance: Evidence-based suggestions for health improvement

Ethical Compliance: This tool follows research ethics guidelines for data collection and privacy protection.

WHO BMI Classification

World Health Organization Standards

Underweight < 18.5 kg/m² Below healthy range
Normal 18.5 – 24.9 kg/m² Healthy weight
Overweight 25 – 29.9 kg/m² Above healthy range
Obese ≥ 30 kg/m² Health risk

Source: World Health Organization (WHO) - International Classification

Data Privacy & Research Ethics

Data Collection Protocol

This research tool collects only height, weight, and calculated BMI values. No personal identifiers (name, email, IP address, location) are collected or stored.

Research Purpose

Data is used solely for aggregate statistical analysis in academic research on population health metrics and BMI distribution patterns.

Consent Framework

Participation is voluntary with explicit opt-in consent. Users can choose not to contribute data while still using the calculation features.

Data Security

All data is encrypted and stored in secure, access-controlled databases. Data retention follows academic research guidelines.

Ethical Compliance

This research follows UII Research Ethics Committee guidelines and international standards for educational research.
